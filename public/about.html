MagRad-CG

Magnetospheric Radiometric Cybernetic Garden

An interactive 3D visualization of Earth's magnetic field and magnetosphere, driven by real geophysical models and historical solar wind observations. All physics runs in the browser — no server, no plugins.

How It Works

The visualization is computed in three layers, stacked from the inside out:

1
Internal field — IGRF-14
Earth's main magnetic field is computed from the International Geomagnetic Reference Field (IGRF-14), a degree-13 spherical harmonic model published by NOAA/IAGA. The app evaluates the magnetic potential at each point and integrates B(r,θ,φ) using fourth-order Runge–Kutta (RK4) to trace field lines from seed points on the surface. The spherical harmonic degree slider lets you see how successive multipoles add complexity to the pure tilted dipole.
2
External field — Tsyganenko T01
Solar wind interaction reshapes the field significantly: dayside field lines are compressed by the solar wind ram pressure; nightside lines are stretched into the long magnetotail. The T01 empirical model (Tsyganenko 2002) captures all major current systems — ring current, magnetotail current sheet, Birkeland currents, Chapman-Ferraro compression, and IMF penetration — in a single closed-form evaluation. It is parameterized by dynamic pressure, Dst, IMF By/Bz, and the G1/G2 storm-history indices from the Qin-Denton database. The Shue (1998) model defines the magnetopause boundary.
3
Historical data playback — OMNI2 + WGhour.d
The timeline at the bottom drives the solar wind inputs from real hourly observations spanning 1963 to the present. Data comes from the NASA OMNI2 merged dataset (solar wind speed, density, IMF By/Bz, Dst) combined with G1 and G2 pre-computed from the Qin-Denton database using Robert Weigel's QinDenton Fortran tool. Monthly JSON files (~28 KB each) are lazy-loaded as you navigate, so only the months you visit are fetched.
Radiation belts — The inner and outer Van Allen belts are shown as L-shell isosurfaces computed from the dipole approximation (L = r / RE cos²λm). The L-shell grids use the internal field only; the dipole L approximation breaks down with external fields in the tail and at the magnetopause.

Physics Models

Internal field

International Geomagnetic Reference Field: the fourteenth generation (IGRF-14)
Alken, P., Thébault, E., Beggan, C.D., et al. (2021 definitive model; IGRF-14 update 2024). IAGA Division V Working Group V-MOD. Published by NOAA NCEI.
ncei.noaa.gov — IGRF product page  ·  IAGA V-MOD working group
Degree/order 1–13 Gauss coefficients used directly.

External field & magnetosphere

A model of the near magnetosphere with a dawn-dusk asymmetry: 1. Mathematical structure
Tsyganenko, N.A. (2002). Journal of Geophysical Research, 107(A8), 1179. doi:10.1029/2001JA000486
T01 model — current implementation. All field-line tracing uses T01.
A model of the near magnetosphere with a dawn-dusk asymmetry: 2. Parameterization and fitting to observations
Tsyganenko, N.A. (2002). Journal of Geophysical Research, 107(A8), 1176. doi:10.1029/2001JA000220
A magnetospheric magnetic field model with a warped tail current sheet (T89c)
Tsyganenko, N.A. (1989). Planetary and Space Science, 37, 5–20. Updated 1992 with dipole-tilt–dependent tail terms.
T89c was the previous implementation; T01 is now used instead.
Magnetopause location under extreme solar wind conditions
Shue, J.H., Song, P., Russell, C.T., Steinberg, J.T., Chao, J.K., Zastenker, G., Vaisberg, O.L., Kokubun, S., Singer, H.J., Detman, T.R., & Kawano, H. (1998). Journal of Geophysical Research, 103(A8), 17691–17700. doi:10.1029/98JA01103
Magnetopause standoff and flaring angle formula used in this app.
A new functional form to study the solar wind control of the magnetopause size and shape
Shue, J.H., Chao, J.K., Fu, H.C., Russell, C.T., Song, P., Khurana, K.K., & Singer, H.J. (1997). Journal of Geophysical Research, 102(A5), 9497–9511. doi:10.1029/97JA00196

Storm-history indices (G1, G2)

Solar wind parameters for magnetospheric magnetic field modeling
Qin, Z., Denton, R.E., Tsyganenko, N.A., & Wolf, S. (2007). Space Weather, 5, S11003. doi:10.1029/2006SW000296
Defines the G1 and G2 disturbance indices used by T01. Pre-computed hourly values are loaded from the Qin-Denton database.

Data Sources

OMNI2 Low-Resolution Solar Wind Data
King, J.H., & Papitashvili, N.E. (2005). "Solar wind spatial scales in and comparisons of hourly Wind and ACE plasma and magnetic field data." Journal of Geophysical Research, 110, A02104. doi:10.1029/2004JA010649
Archive: spdf.gsfc.nasa.gov — OMNI hourly  ·  omniweb.gsfc.nasa.gov
Provides solar wind speed, density, IMF By/Bz, and Dst from 1963 to present. Merged from multiple L1 spacecraft (ACE, Wind, DSCOVR, IMP-8).
Qin-Denton Hourly Database (G1, G2) via QinDenton Fortran tool
Weigel, R. (2024). QinDenton — Fortran tool to compute Qin-Denton parameters from OMNI2 source data. github.com/rweigel/QinDenton
Used to produce WGhour.d, the local source file for G1/G2 and all other solar wind parameters covering 1963 to the present. The output is pre-processed into monthly JSON files by scripts/convert-solarwind.js.
IGRF-14 Gauss Coefficients
National Centers for Environmental Information (NOAA NCEI) / IAGA V-MOD. ncei.noaa.gov
Degree-13 spherical harmonic coefficients for the 2025.0 epoch. Pre-processed into public/data/igrf14coeffs.json by scripts/convert-igrf.js.
NASA Blue Marble — Earth surface texture
NASA/Goddard Space Flight Center. visibleearth.nasa.gov

Software & Libraries

Three.js — 3D WebGL rendering
Cabello, R. (mrdoob) et al. MIT License. threejs.org
Used for the WebGL render loop, TubeGeometry field lines, CatmullRomCurve3 smoothing, marching-cubes isosurfaces, and MeshPhysicalMaterial for semi-transparent belts and magnetopause.
geopack — Python port of Tsyganenko's Fortran code
Tian, S. (tsssss). MIT License. github.com/tsssss/geopack
Used as the implementation reference for the JavaScript ports of both T89c (src/physics/t89.js) and T01 (src/physics/t01.js). Tsyganenko's original Fortran remains the authoritative source; this Python port was used to verify the ported logic against known outputs.

MagRad-CG is an open scientific visualization project. All field-line computation runs client-side in JavaScript. Source code available at GitHub.

Physics models and data are reproduced for educational and research purposes. G1/G2 computation based on the Qin-Denton formula as implemented by the QinDenton tool (Weigel 2024), derived from NASA OMNI2.
